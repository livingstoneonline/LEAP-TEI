As to the further prosecution of this mission
I am very glad to see in your Report since
I began this letter that you still look hopefully
forward. I grieve over the loss this
15country has sustained in the departure of
our brother to his rest. Nine Englishmen
have died in that country since the discovery
of Lake Ngami - One perished by trying to save
a duck in the Zouga - Another, with his servant,
20was lost under suspicion of foul play - Another
ran madly under the feet of an elephant -
and others blew themselves up with gunpowder
the tenth, with his wife, lies at Linyanti &
of the whole he alone had an object worth
25risking his life for. There, without a tree
within a hundred yards to mark the spot, he
rests from his labours, as good and as
devoted a missionary as ever left England.
I collected a few bones & placed him over his
30head - there is not a stone in the country - but
these will soon vanish and his memory
