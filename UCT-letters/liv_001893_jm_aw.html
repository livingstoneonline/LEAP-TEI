                                                        River Shire
                                                        25th February 1863


Sir,


Three Black men who were brought
10from the Cape by the late Bishop MacKenzie to
assist the Universities' Mission are now sent back
to their homes - they were brought up the
Courtesy of the Admiral in a man of war, and
if you can give them a passage down I am au-
15thorized by the Missionaries to say that all expenses
will cheerfully be paid to your Paymaster by the
Bishop of the Cape.


In introducing them, it would scarcely be right in
me not to mention that but little can be said in
20favour of their moral characters & behaviour
while with the Mission.


    Allow me to add, that as the lower Shire
has been completely depopulated by Slave-hunting -
- drought, and famine, we can purchase no
25provisions for the "Pioneer" and will be
                                                                    obliged



The Captain of any Man-ofWar
        touching at Mosambique

obliged to return to the Kongone - Quillimane or
Mozambique for Stores Sooner than we anticipated,
if you can leave any forces at either of these
Ports you will confer a very great favour
5and render us essential Service; as it will
be unwise for us to venture to Johanna at
this Season in this Small Craft.     I have to
beg very Earnestly that you will take this request
into your favourable consideration.


10

    The Shire has not risen as much
as usual this Year, and we have found con=
siderable difficulty in getting the two vessels
up through the Elephant Swamp, but we
hope Soon to reach the Cataracts


15

Our Blacksmith William Mcleod is
obliged by disease of the Kidneys to leave us -
he is a very good man; - we got him from the
"Gorgon" on his passage to the Cape.
